Why A Letter of Hope?

Born from a personal journey through cancer, one letter at a time.

The Heart Behind A Letter of Hope

In February 2024, I was diagnosed with stage 3 cervical and vaginal cancer. In one moment, my entire world shifted. The months that followed were filled with chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy, countless hospital visits, and days when getting out of bed felt impossible.

In the midst of it all, something small but powerful carried me through: letters.

“A handwritten note from a friend arrived on a day I couldn't get out of bed. I kept it beside me, re-reading it on the hard days. It reminded me that I was seen, loved, and not alone — that my story wasn't over yet.”

That single piece of mail sparked something in me. I started writing letters to others going through treatment, and to friends navigating depression and loss. Every time, the response was the same — "This meant more than you know."

I realized that when words feel small, a letter can hold them. When someone feels forgotten, a handwritten note can say: I see you. I'm thinking of you. You are not alone.

And so A Letter of Hope was born — not from a business plan, but from a hospital bed. From the quiet recognition that the smallest gestures often carry the most weight. From the deep belief that no one should walk through a hard season without knowing that someone cares.

Every letter in this collection is written by hand, with intention and prayer. Every envelope is addressed with care. Every package is assembled with the hope that it will arrive on a day when it's needed most — like that first letter did for me.

Whether you're sending a letter to yourself, to a friend, or to someone you've never met, know this: you are part of something beautiful. You are choosing hope. And that choice matters more than you will ever know.

With all my love,
Janice
